Santa Cruz Mountains Long-Term Forest Monitoring Program

Stewardship Research Areas Interactive Explorer Portal

Explore interactive visualizations, ecological information, and data summaries for the 15 Stewardship Research Areas (SRAs) across the Santa Cruz Mountains. The SRAs form the foundation of the Santa Cruz Mountains Long-Term Forest Monitoring Program, a permanent network of 304 monitoring plots established across public, private, and working forestlands. All sites follow standardized data collection methods using the Regional Stewardship Trends Monitoring Protocol. Click and drag to look around each 360° scene; use your mouse or trackpad to zoom in and out. Click on each site page for a plot map, data summary, ecological summary, information on past and planned stewardship, and to request access to the data for use in research or planning.

Big Basin Redwoods State Park

35 plots – Coast redwood forest

View full Big Basin page →

Second-growth coast redwood forest with remnant old-growth patches, affected by the 2020 CZU Complex. Plots are stratified across a burn severity gradient to track forest structure, regeneration, and fuels over time.

Camp Jones Gulch

28 plots - Coast redwood forest

YMCA Camp Jones Gulch plots are located in an old-growth coast redwood forest. This plot network was designed as a before-after-control impact study. Half the sites will have a forest health project implemented in the future, while half are controls. Sampling was conducted in 2025 and the site wil be resamples in 20027 following stewardship activities.

Castle Rock – Black Oak

25 plots - Mixed hardwood / Douglas fir

These plots were collected to evaluate the status of mature black oaks, track encroachment by Douglas fir, and monitor regeneration and recruitment success over time in this rare habitat type. Sites were straified across an area that contains historical and ongoing stewardship activities.

Castle Rock – Hollow

14 plots - Coast redwood and mixed conifer

Castle Rock Hollow is a Sempervirens Fund holding that is adjacent to Castle Rock State Park. The site is characterised as second growth with some protected remnant old-growth redwoods. 14 plots were collected as baseline data to support future stewardship deicion making. The plots were destributed randomly across stands with the tallest trees on the property.

Cotoni Coast Dairies

22 plots – Coast redwood forest

Baseline plots in second-growth coast redwood forests of the newly established Cotoni Coast Dairies National Monument. Some stands burned in the 2020 CZU Complex.
