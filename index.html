Santa Cruz Mountains Long-Term Forest Monitoring Program

Stewardship Research Areas Interactive Explorer Portal

Explore interactive visualizations, ecological information, and data summaries for the 15 Stewardship Research Areas (SRAs) across the Santa Cruz Mountains. The SRAs form the foundation of the Santa Cruz Mountains Long-Term Forest Monitoring Program, a permanent network of 304 monitoring plots established across public, private, and working forestlands. All sites follow standardized data collection methods using the Regional Stewardship Trends Monitoring Protocol. Click and drag to look around each 360° scene; use your mouse or trackpad to zoom in and out.

Big Basin Redwoods State Park

35 plots – Coast redwood forest

Second-growth coast redwood forest with remnant old-growth patches, heavily affected by the 2020 CZU Complex. Plots are stratified across a burn severity gradient to track forest structure, regeneration, and fuels over time.

Camp Jones Gulch

Coast redwood forest

YMCA Camp Jones Gulch monitoring plots track forest conditions in actively used camp and education forests, including fuels, regeneration, and stand structure.

Castle Rock – Black Oak

Mixed hardwood / redwood forest

Plots focused on black oak and associated hardwood–conifer stands, providing baseline data for future fuels treatments and climate adaptation work.

Castle Rock – Hollow

Coast redwood and mixed conifer

Monitoring plots in steep, dissected terrain representing typical interior Santa Cruz Mountains forest conditions.

Cotoni Coast Dairies

22 plots – Coast redwood forest

Baseline plots in second-growth coast redwood forests of the newly established Cotoni Coast Dairies National Monument. Some stands burned in the 2020 CZU Complex; plots capture conditions prior to large-scale restoration and fuels work.

Fall Creek – Henry Cowell

Coast redwood and mixed conifer

Plots track forest recovery and erosion-prone terrain in the Fall Creek Unit, including dense tanoak and mixed conifer stands.

Henry Cowell – Prescribed Burn Unit

Prescribed fire monitoring plots

Monitoring plots established in areas treated with prescribed fire to evaluate fuels reduction, understory response, and regeneration relative to untreated stands.

Mount Madonna

Coast redwood and mixed hardwood

Plots in county park forests capturing a mix of redwood, hardwoods, and legacy tanoak mortality, supporting future fuels and restoration planning.

Nisene Marks – Black Oak

Mixed hardwood / redwood forest

Focused on black oak and associated species to understand composition, structure, and resilience in coastal mixed-hardwood settings.

Nisene Marks – Prescribed Burn

Prescribed fire monitoring plots

Plots established in collaboration with State Parks to monitor fuels, regeneration, and understory response to prescribed fire.

Pescadero Creek

Redwood, Douglas-fir, and hardwoods

Monitoring plots spanning interior coast redwood and Douglas-fir stands in Pescadero Creek County and State Parks.

San Vicente Redwoods

Working forest conservation lands

Plots established in actively managed conservation timberlands to track outcomes of fuels treatments, selective harvest, and restoration activities.

Soquel Demonstration State Forest

Demonstration timber management forest

Monitoring plots in uneven-aged redwood stands and recent harvest units, supporting demonstration of sustainable timber management in the redwood region.

UCSC Forestlands

University research and teaching forest

Plots on UCSC-managed lands support long-term research, teaching, and collaborative stewardship efforts in mixed redwood and hardwood forests.

Wilder Ranch

Coast redwood and coastal terrace forests

Monitoring plots spanning coastal terrace and canyon forests, documenting structure, fuels, and regeneration in State Parks lands adjacent to urban interfaces.
